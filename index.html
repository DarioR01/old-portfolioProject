The prince and the princess

This is a mathematical problem that states:

On the top floor of a castle lives a princess. The floor has n bedrooms arranged in a row. Each bedroom has doors connecting to the adjoining bedrooms as well as to the outside corridor. The princess sleeps in a different bedroom each night by opening the door to an adjoining bedroom and spending the night and the next day in that room. One day a prince arrives at the castle and is desirous of marrying the princess. The guardian angel at the castle tells him of the princess' sleeping patterns and informs him that each morning he may knock on one of the outside doors. If the princess happens to be behind that door, she will open it and consent to marry him. What is the best strategy for the prince to follow?

The problem it self is easy to understand, however it is really hard to figure the best strategy that the prince can use.

I decided that I would need some sample data, and because of that I decided to create a program that simulates the action of the prince and princess in order for me to understand what it is going on in a better way, the simulation data woud have been stored in an excel file where I later could have analysed the data.
